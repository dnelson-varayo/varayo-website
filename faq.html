Unified Communications & VoIP

VoIP, Teams integration, fax support, and voice reliability.

What is VoIP, and how does it differ from traditional phone service? +
VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) transmits calls over the internet instead of traditional copper lines. It offers lower costs, advanced features (like video, messaging, and integrations), and scalability without hardware upgrades.
Do I need to buy new desk phones? +
Not necessarily. Our system is fully softphone-compatible (desktop/mobile apps). If you prefer physical phones, we can often re‑provision existing SIP‑compatible devices — or provide new hardware options.
Can I keep my existing phone numbers when switching? +
Yes — we support seamless number porting in most cases, so your business numbers stay the same.
Do you support fax services with VoIP? +
Absolutely — faxing can be fully integrated. Options include:
  • eFax/digital fax via email, portal, or app (no hardware needed)
  • Fax‑to‑email (incoming faxes arrive as PDFs)
  • Legacy fax machines via ATA support with FoIP (Fax over IP)
What internet speed do I need for reliable VoIP? +
A stable connection with at least ~100 Kbps per concurrent call (up/down) is needed. We recommend business‑grade broadband and can assess your setup during onboarding.
What happens if my internet goes down? +
Calls can fail over to mobile devices or alternate numbers. We also offer redundancy options and proactive monitoring to minimize disruptions.
