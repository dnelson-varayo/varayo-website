Mailing List Policy (Double Opt‑In Required) +

We require all customer-operated mailing lists (or lists referencing resources on our network) to use double opt-in (also called confirmed opt-in) subscription methods to ensure compliance with anti-spam regulations and protect deliverability.

Single Opt‑In (Prohibited)

With single opt-in, an email address is added immediately upon submission. This method is vulnerable to abuse and can result in unsolicited messages. For this reason, we prohibit single opt-in lists on our network.

Double Opt‑In (Required)

The subscriber must confirm their intent via a verification email or link before being added to the list. Unconfirmed addresses receive no further messages. This practice significantly reduces complaints and blacklisting risks.

Recommendations for Effective Double Opt‑In Forms

  • Require users to manually enter their email address.
  • Use an unchecked checkbox for explicit consent.
  • Clearly state: “By opting in, you will receive a confirmation email that you must approve before receiving any future messages.”

Unsubscribe Requirements

All bulk or marketing emails must include a clear, functional unsubscribe mechanism (e.g., a one-click link or reply instructions). Unsubscribe requests must be honored promptly, typically within 24 hours.
